My Works

As a photographer, I would describe the image as a candid moment captured between two people. They sit across from each other at a desk, their faces partially obscured, engrossed in conversation. The lighting is soft, casting gentle shadows on their features.

The individual eyelashes, like fine brushstrokes, frame the eye, hinting at secrets held within. The iris, a kaleidoscope of colors, reveals a hidden galaxy—a nebula of blues, greens, and browns. A tiny catchlight sparkles in the corner, a glimmer of life and consciousness.

The lens would linger, seeking the perfect angle to convey both vulnerability and strength. And then there’s the enigmatic absence, who is she? A daydreamer? A rebel? Or both? Her absence invites us to step into the frame, to become the protagonist of our own story.

A solitary figure stands by an open window, their silhouette framed against the soft glow of twilight. The windowpane, slightly smudged, reveals a world beyond—a world of secrets and possibilities.

The composition balances privacy and curiosity, inviting viewers to wonder about the person’s story. The play of light and shadow adds depth, emphasizing the fleeting nature of this captured moment.

In the dimly lit chamber, a solitary figure emerges. The air hangs with anticipation, as if secrets cling to the very fabric of existence. The subject stands defiantly, their obscured face a canvas of enigma.
